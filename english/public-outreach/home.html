Outreach

Letting the public know what we are doing is an important aspect of the project. Most of this will be performed by the team at the NMS, Nick Fraser, Andy Ross and Stig Walsh, but some will also be done by Roz Wade, who is Education and Outreach Officer at the University Museum of Zoology, Cambridge.

We have already had extensive contacts with the community at Burnmouth, where Jenny Clack gave a well-received talk in July 2011, when the village hall was packed and they had to ship in extra chairs.

On Saturday 10th November, 2012, Jenny spoke about the project to a good audience at the Geological Association Festival of Geology at University College London, and to the Leicester Literary and Philosophical Society on Monday 12th. In July, 2013, she had lots of very positive feedback when she spoke about the project at the 10th International Congress of Vertebrate Morphology in Barcelona.

The Project Blog, which includes a Twitter feed, is maintained by Carys Bennett at the University of Leicester.

We have decided to write an occasional newsletter, and this is currently done by Rob Clack. Please see the Downloads page for links where you can download them.
